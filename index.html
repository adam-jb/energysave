How it works

Simple idea. Big savings.

Electricity prices change every 30 minutes. Your battery charges when it's cheap and powers your home when it's expensive. Fully automated — you don't do anything.

1

Charges at night

Off-peak electricity can cost as little as 7p/kWh. Your battery automatically fills up when rates are at their lowest, typically overnight.

2

Powers your home by day

During peak hours, your home draws from the battery instead of the grid. The system switches seamlessly — you won't notice a thing.

3

Earns while you sleep

Your battery joins our Virtual Power Plant — a network of home batteries that supports the national grid during high demand. This earns revenue that helps keep your subscription low and backs our savings guarantee.

Why now

UK electricity prices have doubled since 2021. A battery breaks the cycle.

Every year, households brace for the next Ofgem price cap announcement. With a battery, the impact of peak rate hikes is dramatically reduced — because you're barely using peak electricity anymore.

Protection from price spikes

When peak rates rise, your exposure shrinks. The battery absorbs most of the impact by shifting your consumption to off-peak hours that stay comparatively cheap.

Less reliance on the grid at its most expensive

Peak electricity is where energy companies make their margin. A battery means you buy less of the product they charge the most for.

Savings grow as prices rise

The bigger the gap between peak and off-peak rates, the more you save. If electricity gets more expensive — and it probably will — your battery becomes more valuable, not less.

Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need solar panels?

No. This system works entirely from the grid. It charges from cheap off-peak electricity and discharges during expensive peak periods. If you do have solar panels, the battery works with those too — storing excess generation for later use.

What electricity tariff do I need?

You'll need to be on the time-of-use tariff we recommend — currently Octopus Agile, which prices electricity in 30-minute windows. This is what creates the price gap the battery exploits. Switching is free and takes minutes; we'll handle it for you. Being on the recommended tariff is a condition of the subscription and the savings guarantee.

What's the contract term?

The minimum term is 5 years. After that, the subscription continues on a rolling monthly basis and you can cancel with 30 days' notice. If you cancel during the first 5 years, an early exit fee applies — £1,500 in year 1, reducing by £300 each year to £0 at year 5. After year 5, if you cancel we remove the battery for a £500 fee.

What if I move house?

The new occupant can take over your subscription. We run the same eligibility check, they sign a transfer form, and the battery stays in place. Estate agents handle similar transfers for solar leases and boiler rentals routinely — a home with lower electricity bills is a selling point. If the new occupant doesn't want the subscription, we remove the battery. The early exit fee applies if you're within the 5-year minimum term; after that, removal costs £500.

Do I own the battery?

No — the battery belongs to EnergySave. You're subscribing to a service: lower electricity bills, guaranteed. We handle the hardware, maintenance, software, and warranty. You pay a fixed monthly fee and save money from day one with zero risk.

How does the savings guarantee work?

Each month, we compare your actual electricity bill to what you would have paid without the battery. If your savings are less than the £49 subscription fee, we refund you the difference. The guarantee requires that you're on the tariff we recommend and that we've assessed your home as suitable before installation. If the maths don't work for your household, we won't install — and you won't be charged.

What is the Virtual Power Plant?

A Virtual Power Plant (VPP) is a network of home batteries that work together to support the national grid during periods of high demand. When the grid needs extra power, your battery contributes small amounts of stored energy for short periods. You won't notice any impact on your home supply. The revenue from the VPP is what allows us to keep the subscription at £49/month and back it with a savings guarantee.

Is it safe?

The battery uses lithium iron phosphate (LFP) chemistry — the safest type of lithium battery available. Installation complies with PAS 63100:2024, the UK's fire safety standard for home battery storage. All installations are registered with your local Distribution Network Operator and carried out by certified installers.

How much will I actually save?

A typical 4-bedroom household (4,500 kWh/year) at the Ofgem price cap pays around £122/month. With a battery on Octopus Agile, the electricity bill drops to roughly £62/month. Including the £49 subscription, your total is £111/month — saving £11/month from day one. Higher-usage households with EVs or heat pumps (6,000+ kWh) save £31+/month. Whatever your situation, the savings guarantee means you'll never pay more with EnergySave than without it.

What maintenance is needed?

None from you. The system is monitored remotely. If anything needs attention, we'll contact you and handle it. All maintenance and repairs are covered by your subscription for its entire duration.

Will it work during a power cut?

The standard configuration is grid-tied and will not power your home during an outage (for safety reasons — to protect grid engineers). Backup power capability is available as an optional upgrade. Ask us during the assessment if this is important to you.
