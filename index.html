How it works

Simple idea. Big savings.

Electricity prices change every 30 minutes. Your battery charges when it's cheap and powers your home when it's expensive. Fully automated — you don't do anything.

1

Charges at night

Off-peak electricity can cost as little as 17p/kWh. Your battery automatically fills up when rates are at their lowest, typically overnight.

2

When peak rates hit 38p/kWh or more, your home draws from the battery instead of the grid. The system switches seamlessly — you won't notice a thing.

Smart software optimises every cycle. Track your savings daily in the app. The battery pays for itself by slashing what you spend on peak electricity.

Why now

UK electricity prices have doubled since 2021. A battery breaks the cycle.

Every year, households brace for the next Ofgem price cap announcement. With a battery, the impact of peak rate hikes is dramatically reduced — because you're barely using peak electricity anymore.

Protection from price spikes

When peak rates rise, your exposure shrinks. The battery absorbs most of the impact by shifting your consumption to off-peak hours that stay comparatively cheap.

Less reliance on the grid at its most expensive

Peak electricity is where energy companies make their margin. A battery means you buy less of the product they charge the most for.

Savings grow as prices rise

The bigger the gap between peak and off-peak rates, the more you save. If electricity gets more expensive — and it probably will — your battery becomes more valuable, not less.

Where the 50% comes from

A typical UK household uses about 58% of electricity during peak hours (38p/kWh on Agile) and 42% off-peak (17p/kWh). With a battery, you shift most of that expensive peak consumption to cheap off-peak rates.

Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need solar panels?

No. This system works entirely from the grid. It charges from cheap off-peak electricity and discharges during expensive peak periods. If you do have solar panels, the battery works with those too — storing excess generation for later use.

What electricity tariff do I need?

You'll need a time-of-use tariff like Octopus Agile, Octopus Go, or Intelligent Octopus. These tariffs charge different rates at different times of day, which is how the battery creates savings. We'll help you switch if needed — it's free and takes minutes.

What happens after 10 years?

You own the battery outright. No more monthly payments. The battery has a 15–20 year expected lifespan, so you'll likely get 5–10 more years of savings with no costs beyond your electricity. At that point, your savings go straight into your pocket.

What if I move house?

You can transfer the agreement to the new homeowner — it's an asset that adds value to the property. Alternatively, if you prefer, we can discuss options for relocating the system or settling the remaining balance.

Is it safe?

The battery uses lithium iron phosphate (LFP) chemistry — the safest type of lithium battery. LFP cells cannot experience thermal runaway (unlike some other lithium chemistries). Installation complies with PAS 63100:2024, the UK's fire safety standard for home battery storage. All installations are registered with your local Distribution Network Operator.

How much will I actually save?

Savings depend on your consumption and tariff, but the typical household on Octopus Agile saves 40–55% on electricity. Our estimate of "half your bill" is based on the UK average consumption split (58% peak / 42% off-peak) and includes an adjustment for the rebound effect — the tendency to use a bit more when prices are lower. We'll give you a personalised estimate during the home assessment.

What maintenance is needed?

None from you. The system is monitored remotely. If anything needs attention, we'll contact you and handle it. All maintenance and repairs are covered in your monthly payment for the full 10 years.

Will it work during a power cut?

The standard configuration is grid-tied and will not power your home during an outage (for safety reasons — to protect grid engineers). Backup power capability is available as an optional upgrade. Ask us during the assessment if this is important to you.
